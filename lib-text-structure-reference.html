Text Structure & Reference

Di soal reading SNBT, memahami struktur teks dan reference words membuat proses membaca jauh lebih cepat. Kamu tidak hanya tahu arti kalimat, tetapi juga memahami bagaimana paragraf dibangun. Materi ini tetap relevan untuk 2026 karena soal literasi Inggris masih sangat bergantung pada inference, paragraph function, dan cohesion.

1. Why text structure matters

Academic and informational texts usually follow a recognizable pattern. If you know the pattern, you can predict where the answer is located and avoid translating every sentence word by word.

Main idea: structure helps you locate purpose, contrast, result, example, and conclusion much faster.

2. Common text structures and signal words

  • Cause – Effect: explains why something happens and what the result is. Signal words: because, therefore, as a result.
  • Problem – Solution: presents an issue, then offers one or more solutions. Signal words: issue, challenge, solve, address.
  • Compare – Contrast: shows similarities and differences. Signal words: however, similarly, unlike, on the other hand.
  • Chronological order: explains events in sequence. Signal words: first, then, finally, afterward.
  • Claim – Evidence: presents an opinion or finding, followed by support or proof.
Exam hint: if a paragraph begins with however, expect a contrast or shift in the writer’s stance.

3. Reference words and how to answer them

Reference questions ask what a word such as it, they, this, these, such, those, former, latter refers to. The answer is usually found just before the reference word appears.

Example: “Many students join study groups because they can exchange ideas.”
The word they refers to many students.
  1. Read the sentence containing the reference word.
  2. Move one clause or one sentence backward.
  3. Find the nearest noun that is logical in meaning and number.
  4. Reinsert that noun into the sentence to check if it makes sense.
Quick rule: do not pick the nearest noun automatically; pick the nearest logical noun.

4. Cohesion and connectors

Connectors help the reader follow the logic of a paragraph.

  • Addition: moreover, furthermore, in addition
  • Contrast: however, nevertheless, on the other hand
  • Cause: because, since, due to
  • Result: therefore, thus, consequently
  • Example: for instance, for example, such as
  • Conclusion: in conclusion, overall, to sum up
Common trap: many students translate every single word, but miss the connector that actually reveals the paragraph’s direction.

5. Fast reading strategy for SNBT 2026

  1. Read the first and last sentence of each paragraph first.
  2. Notice connectors and repeated keywords.
  3. Identify whether the paragraph is explaining, comparing, supporting, or concluding.
  4. Only scan deeper when the question requires detail.
  5. For 2026 preparation, practice identifying main idea, author’s purpose, inference, and reference in one reading pass.

6. Mini practice

Sentence: “Renewable energy is becoming more popular. This trend is driven by lower costs and environmental awareness.”

Question: what does This trend refer to?

Answer: it refers to the increasing popularity of renewable energy.

Extra drill: “Online learning offers flexibility. However, some students still prefer face-to-face interaction.” What is the function of however?

Answer: it signals contrast between the advantage of online learning and the different preference of some students.
