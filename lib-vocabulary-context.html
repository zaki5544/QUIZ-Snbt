🔥 1. The Power of Context Clues

SNBT English will test advanced vocabulary (C1/C2 CEFR level). You do not need a dictionary; the sentence surrounding the word holds the secret. There are 5 types of context clues:

A. Definition Clue

The unfamiliar word is directly followed by its explanation, usually set off by a comma, dash, or "which means".

Example: "The CEO was a misanthrope, a person who deeply distrusted human interaction."
Analysis: The definition is literally given after the comma.

Signal words: which is, that is, in other words, means, refers to, also known as

B. Synonym/Restatement Clue

A nearby word or phrase has a similar meaning to the unknown word.

Example: "The politician's candid remarks surprised everyone; his honest and straightforward response was unusual."
Analysis: "honest and straightforward" restates what "candid" means.

C. Antonym/Contrast Clue

The sentence shows a contrast, meaning the unknown word is the opposite of another word nearby.

Example: "Although his brother was outgoing, he was highly introverted and preferred solitude."
Signal words: although, however, but, unlike, whereas, on the other hand, in contrast, nevertheless, yet, while

D. Example Clue

Specific examples are given that help illustrate the meaning of the word.

Example: "The archaeologist discovered several artifacts, such as ancient pottery, bronze tools, and golden jewelry."
Signal words: such as, for example, for instance, including, like

E. Inference/Logic Clue

No direct definition is given. You must use the overall sentence logic and tone to deduce the meaning.

Example: "After months of drought, the parched farmland yielded almost no harvest."
Analysis: Drought causes dryness → parched = extremely dry.
🛑 Common Trap: SNBT will offer a "common" meaning of a word that doesn't fit the context. For example, "fair" could mean "beautiful," "just," or "light-skinned." Always choose the meaning that fits THIS specific passage!

⚡ 2. Decoding via Roots & Affixes

Many English words are built from Latin and Greek roots. Understanding word parts lets you decode unfamiliar words like a puzzle.

A. Common Prefixes (Awalan)

  • Anti- (against): antibody, antisocial, antidote
  • Pre- (before): predict, precaution, prerequisite
  • Post- (after): postgraduate, postwar, postpone
  • Sub- (under): submarine, subconscious, subordinate
  • Super-/Sur- (above): supernatural, surplus, surpass
  • Mis- (wrong): mislead, misinterpret, misconception
  • Un-/In-/Im-/Il-/Ir- (not): unable, invisible, impossible, illegal, irregular
  • Re- (again): rebuild, reconsider, rewrite
  • Trans- (across): transport, transform, translate
  • Hyper- (excessive): hyperactive, hypersensitive

B. Common Suffixes (Akhiran)

  • -tion/-sion (noun): education, decision, conclusion
  • -ment (noun - result): achievement, development, government
  • -ness (noun - quality): happiness, darkness, awareness
  • -ful (adjective - full of): beautiful, grateful, powerful
  • -less (adjective - without): homeless, careless, meaningless
  • -ous/-ious (adjective): dangerous, ambitious, conscious
  • -able/-ible (adjective - capable): readable, flexible, responsible
  • -ize/-ise (verb - to cause): modernize, realize, emphasize
  • -ly (adverb): quickly, carefully, significantly

C. Key Latin/Greek Roots

  • Spect/Spec (to see): spectator, inspect, perspective, spectrum
  • Dict (to speak): dictation, predict, contradict, verdict
  • Chron (time): chronological, chronic, synchronize
  • Graph/Gram (to write): biography, telegram, photograph
  • Port (to carry): transport, export, portable, report
  • Duct/Duc (to lead): conduct, reduce, introduce, educate
  • Vert/Vers (to turn): introvert, reverse, convert, diverse
  • Bene (good): benefit, benevolent, beneficial
  • Mal (bad): malfunction, malicious, malnutrition
💡 Power Combo: Decode complex words by combining prefix + root + suffix!
Un-predict-able = not + to tell beforehand + capable of = cannot be predicted
Mis-interpret-ation = wrong + to explain + process = wrong explanation

🧠 3. Synonyms Replacement Test

The most common vocabulary question format: "The word 'obsolete' in paragraph 2 is closest in meaning to..."

The 3-Step Replacement Method

  1. Read the original sentence containing the target word and understand its overall meaning/tone.
  2. Replace the target word with each answer option, one by one.
  3. Check if the sentence still makes logical sense and maintains the same tone/meaning.
Passage: "The company's obsolete equipment was replaced with modern technology."

Options:
A. expensive → possible but doesn't explain WHY it was replaced
B. outdated → ✅ makes perfect sense!
C. efficient → contradicts the logic of replacement
D. portable → no logical connection

Answer: B. outdated

Common SNBT Academic Vocabulary

  • Ubiquitous = present everywhere, omnipresent
  • Exacerbate = to make worse, worsen, aggravate
  • Mitigate = to make less severe, reduce, alleviate
  • Pragmatic = practical, realistic, sensible
  • Ambiguous = unclear, open to interpretation, vague
  • Unprecedented = never done before, unparalleled, novel
  • Detrimental = harmful, damaging, injurious
  • Facilitate = to make easier, assist, help
  • Substantial = considerable, significant, large
  • Inherent = built-in, innate, natural, intrinsic
  • Coherent = logical, consistent, well-structured
  • Elicit = to draw out, provoke, evoke
  • Scrutinize = to examine closely, inspect carefully
  • Advocate = to support, champion, promote
  • Compelling = convincing, persuasive, powerful
🛑 Beware of "False Friends": Some words look similar but have different meanings!
• Comprehensive ≠ "comprehensible" (thorough vs. understandable)
• Economic ≠ "economical" (related to economy vs. cost-saving)
• Historic ≠ "historical" (important in history vs. related to history)
• Sensible ≠ "sensitive" (practical vs. easily affected)

💎 4. Idiomatic Phrases & Figurative Meanings

SNBT passages may contain idiomatic expressions, phrasal verbs, or figurative language. The key is to understand them in context, not literally.

A. Common Phrasal Verbs in Academic Texts

  • carry out = to perform, execute ("Scientists carried out the experiment.")
  • come up with = to think of, devise ("She came up with a solution.")
  • look into = to investigate, examine ("Police are looking into the case.")
  • point out = to indicate, highlight ("The report points out several flaws.")
  • give up = to abandon, stop trying ("He gave up his research.")
  • put off = to postpone, delay ("The meeting was put off.")
  • bring about = to cause, result in ("The policy brought about changes.")
  • break down = to analyze/decompose ("Let's break down the data.")
  • rule out = to eliminate, exclude ("Doctors ruled out heart disease.")
  • account for = to explain, constitute ("This accounts for 40% of emissions.")

B. Common Idioms & Fixed Expressions

  • A blessing in disguise = something bad that turns out good
  • The tip of the iceberg = a small visible part of a much larger problem
  • A double-edged sword = something with both advantages and disadvantages
  • Break new ground = to innovate, do something never done before
  • Shed light on = to clarify, explain
  • At the expense of = sacrificing something for another
  • In the wake of = as a consequence of, following
  • Pave the way for = to create conditions for something to happen
💡 Strategy for Unknown Idioms: If you encounter an idiom you've never seen:
1. Read surrounding sentences to determine the tone (positive/negative/neutral).
2. Look at the result or consequence mentioned after the idiom.
3. Choose the answer option that matches the tone and logical flow.

C. Figurative Language

  • Metaphor: "The classroom was a zoo." (chaotic, not literally a zoo)
  • Simile: "She runs like the wind." (very fast)
  • Hyperbole: "I've told you a million times." (many times, not literally)
  • Personification: "The wind whispered through the trees." (wind doesn't whisper)

📚 5. Collocations & Word Families

Collocations are words that naturally go together. SNBT often tests whether you know which words pair correctly.

Common Academic Collocations

  • make a decision / discovery / progress / contribution
  • do research / damage / harm / justice
  • take measures / action / responsibility / into account
  • pay attention / tribute / a price
  • draw a conclusion / attention / a comparison
  • raise awareness / concerns / questions / funds
  • pose a threat / challenge / question / risk
  • reach a consensus / agreement / conclusion
SNBT Question Type: "Which word best completes the sentence?"
"The government needs to ___ immediate action to address climate change."
A. do   B. make   C. take   D. have
Answer: C. take (take action is the correct collocation)

Word Formation Patterns

  • succeed (v) → success (n) → successful (adj) → successfully (adv)
  • create (v) → creation (n) → creative (adj) → creatively (adv)
  • develop (v) → development (n) → developmental (adj)
  • signify (v) → significance (n) → significant (adj) → significantly (adv)
  • analyze (v) → analysis (n) → analytical (adj) → analytically (adv)

🎯 6. Strategi Menjawab SNBT

  1. Never guess from memory alone. Don't select a meaning just because it's the most common dictionary definition. Always verify against the specific context!
  2. Use the Substitution Test. Replace the target word with each answer option. The correct answer will maintain both the meaning AND the tone of the original sentence.
  3. Look for signal words. Words like "however," "although," "for example," "in other words" are powerful clues about the relationship between the unknown word and surrounding text.
  4. Pay attention to word charge. Is the overall paragraph positive, negative, or neutral? The unknown word's meaning should match this tone.
  5. Break down unfamiliar words. Use your knowledge of prefixes, roots, and suffixes to make educated guesses.
  6. Eliminate obviously wrong options first. Usually 2 out of 4/5 options can be eliminated quickly, making your final choice easier.
💡 Time Management: Vocabulary questions should take 30-45 seconds each. If you're stuck for more than 1 minute, mark your best guess and move on. Your subconscious will continue processing!
